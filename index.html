About

Short facts

Currently I work at the FEMTO-ST lab in Besançon, France. The main research interest is in the field of reservoir computing, in particular, Nonlinear Transient Computing (NTC). The story began in May 2012, at the conference in Mellas, Crimea, Ukraine where I attended a talk of my future supervisor. Also, I am participating in a robotics contest in France. Technologies used: FPGA and Haskell.

I do support an idea of open source. What is appealing personally to me is the ‘license’ that not only allows, but encourages the constructive criticism (hence, the source is open). In my own vision, open source is sometimes a creativity and exclusivity that you can afford instead of commercial perfectness and mass production.

At present, I do adore functional programming paradigm. But it was now always so. I had heard a lot about Haskell programming language, but never tried it until my eye occasionally caught a monospaced advertisement on the bulletin board at UKMA where I was a Master student. That was Semen who later offered to participate in Learn You a Haskell book translation, the first open source (or at least closely related to) project I joined. In my spare time, I participate at Slovnenya project, Ukrainian-multilingual dictionary.

I wanted to see Himalayas since I was eight. Suddenly, the dream became real. Twelve years later. It was a backpack trip to India. And a chance to get lost with a taxi driver following the wrong cars right the first night in a 22 million city to visit several Buddhist places (the residence of Dalai Lama in Dharamshala, stupas in Bodh Gaya, Rajgir, Vaishali, etc.), the Golden Temple, as well as Hindu funerals in Varanasi. Subsequently, there were Dead Sea and Jerusalem in Israel; cuisine of Gaziantep, hot Antalya, ancient mosaics in Turkey; Indonesia with its volcanoes, tropical rains, corals, too much of ayam goreng and lots of islands. And other countries. Now I stay in one of the beautiful parts of Europe where I am a PhD student in photonics. Below you will find the video of that trip to India along with some other interests.
